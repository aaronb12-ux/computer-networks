## Delays

A packet suffers different types of delay when it is being transmitted across the network:
- Processing delay
- Queuing delay
- Transmission delay
- Propagation delay

![Screenshot 2026-04-28 at 8 13 20 PM](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/d0da9382-fc3e-48e5-b914-f19ae781f577)

**Processing Delay:** Performing checksum for each packet's bits, etc.

**Queuing Delay:** Packets wait to be transmitted. Any time a packet spends waiting in this queue is queuing delay.

**Transmission Delay:** L / R, happens at a node. Time it takes to put a packet's bits on a link.

**Propagation Delay:** Happens on the link itself, the physical amount of time it needs to travel from one place to another.

## Packet Loss

- Queue preceding link in buffer has finite capacity
- When the buffer gets full, packets can get lost

![Screenshot 2026-04-28 at 8 18 03 PM](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/bb365f82-5d2f-434d-a806-7e0b0ba3dd81)

The ideal case is that the node can transmit packets faster than the rate at which packets arrive (A). So an ideal case is **(L / R) > A**.

In this case, all the packets get sent out. The queuing delay is also 0 in this case.

In the case **L/R < A**, the buffer begins filling.

## Bandwidth Delay Product

The bandwidth delay product (BDP) is the maximum amount of data (in bits or bytes) that can be "in flight" or in transit within a network link at any given moment.

**BDP = bandwidth × RTT**

## Jitter

Jitter in networking is the variability or inconsistency in latency (delay) of data packets arriving at their destination. While latency is the total time for data transmission, jitter measures how much that time fluctuates, often causing choppy audio, robotic voices, or frozen video in real-time applications.
